WHO
Target Audience

Primary Audience

This training initiative targets Department of Defense Mentor Protégé Program Managers. They manage their Mentor Protégé programs at their DoD Services or Agencies. These personnel require an in depth knowledge of the DFARS Appendix I, the MPP SOP and other Regulatory Guidance to fulfill their roles and responsibilities effectively.

Key Stakeholder Groups

  • Mentor Protégé Program Managers - Primary beneficiaries gaining critical MPP competency
  • Mentor Organizations - Receive improved support and expedited approvals
  • Protégé Firms - Benefit from knowledgeable oversight and guidance
  • OSBP Personnel - Experience reduced administrative burden and improved efficiency
  • Contracting Officers - Gain partners with enhanced MPP expertise
  • Small Business Specialists - Collaborate with better-informed MPP Program Managers

Training Development Team

Subject matter experts from the Office of Small Business Programs developed this curriculum based on extensive regulatory expertise, operational experience, and documented performance gaps requiring institutional remediation.

📚
WHAT
Training Content

Comprehensive Curriculum

The training program addresses all critical aspects of Mentor-Protégé Program administration required for effective MPP Program Manager performance:

  • MPP Standard Operating Procedures - Complete operational protocols and procedural frameworks
  • DFARS Appendix I Requirements - Detailed regulatory compliance standards and obligations
  • MPP Program Manager Responsibilities - Managing agreements and financial accountability; coordinating policy implementation and real-time oversight; strategic program alignment and performance improvement
  • Agreement Review Protocols - Step-by-step evaluation and approval processes
  • Compliance Verification Methods - Techniques for ensuring regulatory adherence
  • Common Deficiencies - Frequently identified errors and preventive strategies
  • Best Practice Standards - Proven methodologies from successful program implementations
  • Case Study Analysis - Real-world scenarios and decision-making frameworks

Instructional Methodology

Content delivery employs professional learning principles through interactive modules combining video instruction, regulatory document analysis, practical exercises, knowledge assessments, and job aids designed for immediate workplace application.

⏰
WHEN
Application Timeline

Recommended Completion Requirement

MPP Program Managers should complete this training within 30 days of assignment to appointed position. This is also an annual requirement. This ensures baseline competency exists prior to executing oversight responsibilities.

Critical Application Points

MPP Program Managers apply acquired knowledge throughout the acquisition lifecycle:

  • MPA Planning Phase - Evaluating MPP opportunity identification and integration
  • Pre-Award Activities - Reviewing proposed Mentor-Protégé agreement submissions
  • Agreement Coordination - Facilitating OSBP approval processes and documentation
  • Post-Award Administration - Monitoring agreement implementation and performance
  • Annual Compliance Reviews - Assessing program adherence and effectiveness
  • Modification Processing - Evaluating proposed changes to existing agreements
  • Closeout Procedures - Ensuring proper agreement closeout protocol

Time Investment

The program requires approximately 20 hours of self-paced learning. Participants may complete modules incrementally with automatic progress tracking and session resumption capabilities.

WHERE
Delivery Platform

Online Learning Infrastructure

Training is delivered through a secure, web-based learning management system providing 24/7 accessibility from any internet-connected location, enabling flexible completion around operational schedules.

Technical Specifications

  • Platform Architecture - Web-based application compatible with all modern browsers
  • Device Compatibility - Optimized for desktop, laptop, and tablet devices. Use of smartphones is not recommended.
  • Network Requirements - Standard internet connectivity (NIPR/unclassified access)
  • Authentication Method - CAC/PIV card or DoD-approved credential systems
  • Progress Tracking - Automated completion monitoring and reporting
  • Certificate Generation - Automated training completion documentation

Workplace Integration

MPP Program Managers apply learned competencies directly within their operational environments during contract reviews, agreement evaluations, stakeholder coordination, and program oversight activities across organizational units.

Supplemental Resources

Reference materials, decision matrices, procedural job aids, and OSBP subject matter expert contact information are accessible within the platform and available for download to individual workspaces.

🎯
WHY
Strategic Justification

Mission-Critical Requirement

The Mentor-Protégé Program serves as a strategic tool for strengthening defense industrial base resilience. MPP Program Manager competency directly impacts program effectiveness, small business capability development, and acquisition mission success.

Regulatory Compliance Mandate

DFARS Appendix I establishes mandatory requirements governing MPP administration. Current MPP Program Manager knowledge deficiencies create significant compliance risks, audit exposure, and potential legal liability requiring immediate institutional intervention.

Operational Efficiency Gap

Performance data demonstrates that preventable errors attributable to knowledge gaps can cause MPP agreement processing delays. These inefficiencies impose unnecessary burden on stakeholders and undermine program credibility.

Documented Performance Issues

  • Agreement Rejections - High rates of submission errors requiring rework
  • Processing Delays - Extended review cycles due to incomplete documentation
  • Compliance Findings - Audit deficiencies related to oversight gaps
  • Stakeholder Friction - Concerns expressed from Mentors and Protégés
  • OSBP Workload - Excessive time spent on reactive troubleshooting

Strategic Outcomes

This training initiative addresses root causes rather than symptoms, establishing sustainable institutional capability that ensures MPP Program Managers possess requisite knowledge to execute their responsibilities effectively, efficiently, and in full regulatory compliance.

BENEFITS
Training Benefits & Outcomes

Measurable Organizational Benefits

Implementation of this training program delivers quantifiable improvements across multiple performance dimensions:

  • Compliance Assurance - Ensures Department-wide adherence to DFARS requirements and MPP SOP protocols
  • Processing Efficiency - Reduces agreement review timelines through error elimination
  • Administrative Relief - Decreases OSBP staff burden through reduced reactive support
  • Audit Readiness - Minimizes compliance findings and regulatory concerns
  • Stakeholder Satisfaction - Improves Mentor and Protégé experience through knowledgeable oversight
  • Program Credibility - Enhances overall MPP operations and solidifies the permanency of the MPP program

Individual Performance Enhancement

MPP Program Managers completing this training demonstrate improved capabilities:

  • Regulatory Knowledge - Comprehensive understanding of DFARS Appendix I requirements
  • Procedural Competency - Proficiency in MPP SOP application and execution
  • Risk Management - Ability to identify and mitigate compliance vulnerabilities
  • Decision Confidence - Enhanced judgment in agreement evaluation scenarios
  • Career Development - Specialized expertise valuable for professional advancement

Strategic Mission Impact

Beyond immediate operational improvements, this initiative strengthens the defense industrial base through more effective Mentor-Protégé partnerships, enhanced small business capability development, and improved acquisition outcomes supporting national security objectives.

Institutional Sustainability

The training establishes scalable, repeatable infrastructure ensuring all current and future MPP Program Managers receive consistent, high-quality education regardless of component affiliation, geographic location, or assignment timing.
