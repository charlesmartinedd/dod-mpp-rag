Executive Overview

Comprehensive Mentor-Protégé Program Training for Program Managers

Executive Summary

The Department of Defense Office of Small Business Programs proposes the immediate implementation of a comprehensive, standardized training program for Program Managers regarding their Mentor-Protégé Program (MPP) responsibilities, Standard Operating Procedures (SOP), and DFARS Appendix I compliance requirements.

This initiative addresses a critical institutional gap that currently undermines program effectiveness, increases compliance risk, and imposes unnecessary administrative burden on oversight personnel. The proposed training program will establish baseline competency, standardize procedures, and enable Program Managers to fulfill their regulatory obligations with confidence and precision.

Key Imperatives

  • Compliance Mandate: Ensure Department-wide adherence to DFARS requirements and SOP protocols
  • Risk Mitigation: Eliminate preventable errors resulting from knowledge gaps
  • Operational Efficiency: Reduce agreement processing delays and administrative rework
  • Institutional Capability: Build sustainable Program Manager competency infrastructure
  • Mission Readiness: Strengthen small business industrial base through effective program execution

Expected Outcomes

01

Immediate Competency Enhancement

Within 90 days of program launch, 100% of active Program Managers will complete baseline MPP training, establishing minimum competency standards and shared understanding of regulatory requirements.

02

Reduced Administrative Burden

OSBP staff time dedicated to reactive Program Manager support and error remediation will decrease by 50%, enabling reallocation to strategic program development and stakeholder engagement activities.

03

Accelerated Agreement Processing

Average MPP agreement review and approval timelines will improve by 40% through elimination of common submission errors, incomplete documentation, and procedural non-compliance requiring rework.

04

Enhanced Audit Readiness

Department-wide MPP audit findings related to Program Manager oversight deficiencies will decrease to near-zero levels, demonstrating effective institutional knowledge transfer and compliance capability.

05

Sustainable Infrastructure

Establishment of evergreen training curriculum with annual updates ensures ongoing Program Manager education aligns with regulatory changes, policy updates, and emerging best practices.

Path Forward

Executive approval and resource commitment are required to initiate this critical capability development program. Immediate action positions the Department to address current gaps while establishing sustainable infrastructure for long-term program excellence.
